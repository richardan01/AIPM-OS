# Jurisdiction scope skill (knowledge)

**Type:** Knowledge — scope reference  
**Purpose:** What is in-scope vs out-of-scope for HK regulatory evaluation. Consult when the text references offshore entities, foreign regulators, or cross-border arrangements.

---

## In-scope (evaluate against HK standards)

| Regime | Regulatory body | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Securities and futures | SFC | All SFO-regulated activities in HK |
| Banking | HKMA | Authorised institutions operating in HK |
| Insurance | Insurance Authority (IA) | Insurance products distributed in HK |
| AML/CFT | HKMA / SFC | AMLO obligations for HK-licensed entities |
| Personal data | PCPD | PDPO obligations for data collected in HK |
| Virtual assets | SFC (post-June 2023) | VASP licensing for exchanges serving HK retail |
| Listed companies | SFC / HKEX | Listing Rules, continuous disclosure |
| MPF / pensions | MPFA | Mandatory provident fund requirements |

---

## Out-of-scope (fire trigger 4 if obligation is EXCLUSIVELY from these regimes)

| Jurisdiction | Regulator(s) |
|---|---|
| Singapore | MAS |
| United States | SEC, CFTC, FINRA, OCC |
| United Kingdom | FCA, PRA |
| European Union | ESMA, national NCAs (BaFin, AMF, etc.) |
| Australia | ASIC, APRA |
| Japan | FSA Japan |
| Mainland China | CSRC, PBOC, CBIRC (unless PRC-specific cross-border rule applies) |
| Canada | OSC, CSA |

**Trigger 4 fires ONLY when:** The obligation being evaluated is exclusively a foreign regime obligation and cannot be evaluated against HK standards from the text alone.

---

## Cross-border edge cases (consult before firing trigger 4)

### HK entity offering products offshore
- If a HK-licensed entity is offering products TO foreign clients, HK licensing (SFO, HKMA authorisation) is still the primary in-scope regime for the HK entity's conduct.
- Whether foreign jurisdiction's rules are satisfied is OUT-OF-SCOPE and fires trigger 4.

### Foreign entity offering into HK
- If a foreign entity offers products to HK retail investors without SFC authorisation → in-scope, potentially non-compliant under SFO Part IV.
- If the foreign entity's home-jurisdiction compliance is being assessed → out-of-scope.

### ICMA / international standards (ESG, green bonds, etc.)
- ICMA Green Bond Principles: not a HK regulatory requirement on their own. HK-specific ESG adequacy is assessed against SFC and HKMA circulars.
- If text claims ICMA compliance but HKMA/SFC ESG standard compliance cannot be verified from text → fire trigger 5a (unclear, evidence insufficient), not trigger 4.

### Mainland China cross-border data (PDPO)
- Data transfers FROM HK to PRC: in-scope under PDPO — fire trigger 5b or 2 as appropriate, not trigger 4.
- PRC data laws (Cybersecurity Law, PIPL) applicable to PRC entity: out-of-scope.

### AMLO and cross-border banking
- AMLO obligations apply to HK-authorised institutions regardless of where their clients are domiciled.
- A HK private bank servicing offshore clients: AML/CDD obligations are IN-SCOPE.
- Whether foreign CDD rules are satisfied: out-of-scope → trigger 4 for the foreign-law component only.

---

## Quick test — is this trigger 4?

Ask three questions:
1. Is there a HK regulatory obligation that can be assessed from the text? → If YES, do not fire trigger 4 for that obligation.
2. Is the only obligation at issue a foreign-regime obligation? → If YES, fire trigger 4.
3. Is it a mixed case? → Label unclear (trigger 4) only if the HK element CANNOT be evaluated independently of the foreign-regime component.
