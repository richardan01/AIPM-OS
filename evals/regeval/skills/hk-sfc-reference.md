# HK SFC regulatory reference skill (knowledge)

**Type:** Knowledge — domain reference  
**Purpose:** Key HK regulatory obligations, their instruments, and what compliant conduct looks like. Consult when identifying whether an obligation is known and whether it is satisfied.

## Key obligation patterns (what compliant looks like)

### Suitability (SFO / SFC Code of Conduct Principle 5)
- Suitability assessment MUST precede any investment recommendation
- Applies to all solicited AND unsolicited recommendations on securities products
- **Compliant signal:** "Before recommending... we are required to assess..." + no specific product pitched without prior assessment
- **Non-compliant signal:** Specific product recommendation without suitability caveat, especially to an unnamed/generic customer

### Licensing (SFO regulated activities)
- Type 1: Dealing in securities
- Type 4: Advising on securities
- Type 5: Advising on futures contracts
- Type 9: Asset management
- Criminal liability for unlicensed Type 4 activity regardless of how the activity is characterised
- **Compliant signal:** Correct citation of licence type + referral to SFC register
- **Non-compliant signal:** Claim that general commentary / no direct money management exempts from Type 4

### AML / CDD (AMLO + HKMA AML Guideline)
- CDD mandatory at account opening AND ongoing monitoring required
- UBO: identify all beneficial owners at ≥ 25% threshold
- UBO disclosure is NOT optional for corporate accounts
- STR obligation cannot be waived by customer relationship tenure
- **Non-compliant signal:** Suggesting transaction structuring to circumvent AMLO HK$120k reporting threshold; waiving CDD for long-standing customers; treating UBO as optional

### Market misconduct (SFO Part XIII)
- Insider dealing: trading on material non-public information
- Layering/spoofing: manipulative order placement patterns
- **Non-compliant signal:** Advising on MNPI trades; endorsing order patterns with 200:1 order-to-fill ratio without SFC engagement

### CIS public offering (SFO Part IV)
- All collective investment schemes require SFC authorisation for public offering in HK
- Professional investor exemption exists but must be explicitly invoked
- Offshore / Cayman funds → non-compliant if offered to retail without authorisation

### Cooling-off periods
- ILAS products: 21 calendar days (IA requirement)
- Must be clearly stated including commencement and expiry date
- Blank commencement date → unclear (trigger 2)

### Compliant educational output (trigger 1 exclusion)
Educational or general content is **compliant** if it explicitly includes at least one of:
- (a) "Not personalised advice" disclaimer WITH referral to a SFC-licensed adviser
- (b) Appropriate refusal to provide regulated advice
- (c) Required past-performance caveats (where applicable)

Content with all three absent AND no other regulatory obligation satisfied → **non-compliant** if a specific recommendation is made, or **unclear** if no obligation is identifiable.

### Fair disclosure / representations (SFO s.277 + SFC conduct)
- Prohibited: False/misleading statements about investment products
- Return guarantees, principal protection claims, and specific performance promises are per se violations unless backed by a principal guarantee structure
- **Non-compliant signal:** "Guarantees 8% return", "principal protected", "zero risk"

### Research analyst standards (SFC Code — conflicts)
- Conflict disclosures must address: proprietary holdings, pending mandates, analyst personal holdings
- Historic underwriting alone is insufficient if current conflicts exist
- **Unclear signal:** Partial disclosure (historical only) without addressing current positions

### PDPO cross-border data transfer
- Transfer of HK personal data to mainland China raises PDPO adequacy questions
- Blanket ToS consent is a contested mechanism for cross-border transfers
- **Unclear signal:** Consent buried in ToS; no adequacy assessment mentioned

### VASP licensing (post-June 2023)
- After AMLO amendments (effective 1 June 2023): ALL virtual asset exchanges serving HK retail require VASP licence from SFC, regardless of whether they list security tokens
- Outputs describing pre-June 2023 opt-in framework as current → materially outdated → non-compliant or unclear depending on context
