## Result narrative

The judge achieved perfect accuracy on both the `compliant` set (10/10) and the `non-compliant` set (10/10). It scored 0/10 on `unclear` items — not because it made random errors, but because the abstention policy ("do NOT use unclear as a hedge... prefer a weak label over an absent one") caused the judge to emit zero unclear labels across the entire run.

The entire κ gap from 1.0 to 0.5 lives in the unclear class. Per-class κ for unclear = 0.000 because the judge never used the unclear label (judge marginal U=0). The scaffold and the gold panel are operating with different definitions of when to abstain.

Two structural explanations:
1. **Rubric is too narrow on unclear:** "cannot determine regulation" or "text so incomplete" are stricter criteria than what the gold panel used (which also applied unclear to borderline calls, ambiguous framing, and outdated-law scenarios)
2. **Some gold=unclear items arguably have directional violations** — reg-0024 (citation misstatement), reg-0025 (unsubstantiated performance claims), reg-0026 (outdated VASP law), reg-0030 (vague compliance boast) all have material defects the judge correctly detected; gold labeled them unclear for different reasons (ambiguity about severity, knowledge-cutoff vs. misstatement, etc.)

This is not a model failure. It is a calibration mismatch between the rubric and the gold panel's labeling practice.

## Why this verdict

κ=0.500 beats placeholder champion (κ=0) but misses the KEEP threshold of 0.80; all 10 mismatches are gold=unclear items where the scaffold's anti-hedge abstention policy forces directional labels that the gold panel marked borderline.

## Next experiment suggestion

**baseline-v2-unclear-expanded**: relax the unclear definition to include "material citation imprecision, outdated regulatory statement, or vague/unsubstantiated compliance claim" — this should recover 3–4 of the 10 unclear mismatches (reg-0024, reg-0026, reg-0030, potentially reg-0025) without degrading TPR/TNR.

## κ computation workings

**Confusion matrix (rows = gold, cols = judge):**

|  | judge=compliant | judge=non-compliant | judge=unclear |
|---|---|---|---|
| gold=compliant | 10 | 0 | 0 |
| gold=non-compliant | 0 | 10 | 0 |
| gold=unclear | 6 | 4 | 0 |

**N = 30**  
**p_o = (10+10+0) / 30 = 20/30 = 0.667**

**Marginals:**
- Gold: compliant=10, non-compliant=10, unclear=10
- Judge: compliant=16, non-compliant=14, unclear=0

**p_e = (10×16 + 10×14 + 10×0) / 30² = 300/900 = 0.333**

**κ = (0.667 − 0.333) / (1 − 0.333) = 0.333 / 0.667 = 0.500**

**SE(κ) ≈ sqrt[ p_o·(1−p_o) / (N·(1−p_e)²) ] = sqrt[ 0.667·0.333 / (30·0.444) ] = sqrt[0.0167] = 0.129**

**95% CI: [0.500 − 1.96×0.129, 0.500 + 1.96×0.129] = [0.247, 0.753]**
