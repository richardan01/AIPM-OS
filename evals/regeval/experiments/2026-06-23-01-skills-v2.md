# RegEval experiment 2026-06-23-01 — skills-v2

**Verdict:** HOLD
**κ:** 0.822 (95% CI [0.721, 0.917])  vs champion 0.820
**Diagnostics:** TPR=0.976 · TNR=0.950 · abst=0.120 · N=100
**Wall clock:** 3:04 (workflow, 10 parallel judge agents)

## Hypothesis
skills-v1 regressed on 7 compliant items because Step 4 treated accurate descriptions of regulatory frameworks/processes as unconfirmed compliance claims, firing trigger 5a. Adding one exclusion clause — distinguishing *describing* a process from *asserting completion of* a process — will recover those items and push κ above 0.820.

## Scaffold delta vs champion (rubric-graded-v4)
Inherits skills-v1's 6-step workflow + skills/jurisdiction/edge-case references. Single-clause patch to Step 4: a "Description exclusion" — if the text accurately *describes* a framework/obligation without asserting the entity has satisfied it, Step 4 does not fire trigger 5a. Everything else identical to skills-v1.

## Result narrative
The patch worked on its target. TPR rose from skills-v1's 0.786 to 0.976 — 6 of the 7 compliant items skills-v1 lost to trigger-5a over-fire (reg-0007, reg-0037, reg-0062, reg-0064, reg-0083, reg-0086) are now correct. Only reg-0054 still abstains (judge fired trigger 2/5a on a CIES "fully satisfies requirements" assertion — a defensible read). Compliant per-class κ = 0.898; unclear per-class κ improved to 0.611 (vs champion ~0.537). The 11 remaining mismatches are concentrated in the gold=unclear class (the judge commits to compliant/non-compliant on genuinely indeterminate items) — the same hard class the champion also struggles with.

## Why this verdict
κ=0.822 meets all three KEEP conditions mechanically (κ≥0.80, CI lower 0.721≥0.70, abst<0.50). Recorded HOLD rather than KEEP for two reasons: (1) +0.002 over champion is within noise — the CIs overlap almost entirely ([0.721,0.917] vs [0.720,0.920]); (2) cross-model confound — this run's judges were Opus-4.8 subagents (parallel-agent method), whereas the champion κ=0.820 was scored on claude-sonnet-4-5 via run_experiment.py. The description-exclusion fix is validated as a direction; promotion to champion is deferred pending a same-model (sonnet-4-5 API) confirmation run or an explicit promotion decision. current.md unchanged.

## Cross-model note
This is a second cross-model data point alongside fable-judge-v1 (κ=0.677 on Fable-5). skills-v2 on Opus-4.8 lands at κ=0.822, comparable to rubric-graded-v4 on Sonnet-4-5 (0.820) — a mild robustness signal for the scaffold across model families, NOT a like-for-like improvement over the champion.

## Next experiment suggestion
Confirmation run of skills-v2 on claude-sonnet-4-5 (run_experiment.py, same model as champion) to remove the cross-model confound and decide promotion on a like-for-like κ.
